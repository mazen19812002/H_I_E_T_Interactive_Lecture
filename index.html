Introduction & Concepts

Quality assurance in higher education requires precise definition of teaching, learning, and assessment strategies linked to learning outcomes (NARS 2018). These strategies are crucial for program success.

General Concepts

  • Teaching: An intentional, planned process by faculty to help students achieve learning outcomes (knowledge, skills, behaviors).
  • Learning: Self-activity by the student (supervised or not) to acquire knowledge, skill, or behavior change. It is the result of practice and experience. Distinction: Teaching is facilitation by a qualified person. Learning is the student's own effort to acquire knowledge.

Learning Styles (VARK)

Active learning transforms the student from a passive receiver to an active participant. People remember 10% of what they read, but 90% of what they do.

The VARK Model

  • Visual: Learn via images, maps, charts, diagrams. High imagination.
  • Auditory: Learn via sound, lectures, discussions, and conversation.
  • Read/Write: Most common. Rely on continuous reading, writing, and rewriting notes.
  • Kinesthetic: Learn via movement, practical training, experiments, and trial.

Teaching Strategies

H.I.E.T adopts "Student-Centered" strategies mostly, with some "Lecturer-Centered" methods where necessary.

1. Lecturer-Centered Strategies

Interactive Lecture: Involves students by asking for opinions...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Introduce the topic with a clear overview.
  • Ask provocative questions to elicit opinions/predictions.
  • Ensure every student participates at least once.
  • Monitor engagement and adjust pace accordingly.
Guided Field Trips: Moving class to a real site...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Select a site (e.g., factory) relevant to the syllabus.
  • Brief students on safety and learning objectives before travel.
  • Guide students to observe real-world applications of theory.
  • Conduct a debriefing session to match practice with theory.

2. Student-Centered Strategies

Online Self-Learning: Students study assigned material alone...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Assign specific reading or digital material.
  • Students study independently before the class.
  • Use class time solely for discussion and answering deep questions.
  • Focus on developing analysis and self-confidence.
Debate & Discussion: Verbal dialogue management...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Pose a controversial or complex question.
  • Students prepare arguments for the next session.
  • Manage a structured verbal dialogue/debate in class.
  • Encourage expression of diverse opinions and confidence.
Reporting/Research: Writing reports on topics...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Define a specific research topic related to the course.
  • Students conduct research and write a structured report.
  • Focus on professional writing, image usage, and analysis.
  • Evaluate based on depth of research and clarity.
Problem-Based Learning: Analyzing and solving a problem...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Present a real-world problem to the students.
  • Students analyze the problem and deduce root causes.
  • Students search for and propose multiple solutions.
  • Evaluate and compare solutions to find the best fit.
Experiential Learning: Performing lab/field experiments...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Set up a lab or field experiment.
  • Students perform the task and monitor inputs.
  • Students record outputs and observe changes.
  • Deduce relationships and conclusions from the data.

3. Cooperative Learning

Brainstorming: Generating creative ideas...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Introduce a problem or topic to the group.
  • Encourage rapid generation of ideas without judgment.
  • Focus on quantity and idea fluency over logic initially.
  • Review and refine ideas in a secondary phase.
Collaborative Projects: Groups work on tasks...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Form small, diverse groups of students.
  • Assign a complex task or project.
  • Students organize roles and manage their team.
  • Evaluate both the final product and team dynamics.
Presentations: Using tools to present facts...
Detailed Implementation Steps:
  • Students prepare electronic slides (images, video).
  • Students present facts and concepts to peers.
  • Allows for self-paced learning and review.
  • Enhances communication and technical skills.

Demonstration Strategy: Instructor demonstrates a skill/process...
Description:

A teaching method in which the instructor actively demonstrates a skill or process to students to provide a visual model of execution, enhancing understanding through observation.

Implementation Steps:
  • Explain the purpose and theory behind the skill.
  • Demonstrate the skill slowly and clearly step-by-step.
  • Allow students to practice under supervision.
  • Provide immediate feedback and correct errors.
Advantages:
  • Active learning & Visual learning.
  • Personalized feedback on errors.
  • Enhanced retention of practical skills.
Disadvantages:
  • Limited scope (can't demo everything).
  • Time-consuming for individual support.
  • Limited student engagement if topic is dry.
Blended Learning: Combines traditional classroom with digital learning...
Description:

An approach that mixes traditional face-to-face classroom methods with online digital media and tasks, giving students some control over time, place, path, or pace.

Implementation Steps:
  • Analyze the curriculum to decide which parts are best online vs. offline.
  • Select or create digital materials (videos, quizzes).
  • Design face-to-face activities that deepen online learning.
  • Integrate assessments that cover both modes.
Advantages:
  • Flexibility & Personalization.
  • Improved student engagement via variety.
  • Cost-effective relative to pure classroom.
Disadvantages:
  • Technical issues & Access to technology.
  • Requires additional instructor training.
  • Limited access for some students.
WebQuest: Inquiry-based activity using internet resources...
Description:

An inquiry-oriented lesson format in which most or all the information that learners work with comes from the web.

Implementation Steps:
  • Define the topic and the specific task/challenge.
  • Curate a list of reliable web resources for students.
  • Create a process guide with clear roles/steps.
  • Students synthesize information and present findings.
Advantages:
  • Promotes student-centered & collaborative learning.
  • Enhances critical thinking & analysis.
  • Increases engagement via interactivity.
Disadvantages:
  • Technology/Connectivity issues.
  • Time-consuming research process.
  • Requires very clear instructions.
Flipped Classroom: Watch content at home, do active learning/problem solving in class...
Description:

A strategy that reverses the traditional learning environment by delivering instructional content, often online, outside of the classroom.

Implementation Steps:
  • Record lectures or select video content for home viewing.
  • Share content with students to watch before class.
  • Use class time for problem-solving and active discussion.
  • Provide immediate guidance during practice.
Advantages:
  • Increased engagement during class time.
  • Personalized learning pace at home.
  • More teacher-student interaction during practice.
Disadvantages:
  • Relies heavily on technology access at home.
  • Time-consuming preparation for teachers.
  • Potential confusion for students new to the model.
Differentiated Instruction: Tailoring instruction to diverse needs...
Description:

Tailoring instruction to meet individual needs by differentiating content, process, products, or the learning environment.

Implementation Steps:
  • Assess students' readiness, interests, and learning profiles.
  • Modify content (what is learned) or process (how it is learned).
  • Provide different options for final product (assessment).
  • Use flexible grouping strategies.
Advantages:
  • Increases engagement by meeting individual needs.
  • Promotes inclusive classrooms.
  • Supports personalized learning paths.
Disadvantages:
  • Extremely time-consuming to design.
  • Requires extensive planning.
  • Assessment can be complex.
Programmed Instruction: Self-paced linear sequential learning...
Description:

A method of presenting new subject matter to students in a graded sequence of controlled steps.

Implementation Steps:
  • Break down content into small, manageable frames.
  • Arrange frames in a logical, linear sequence.
  • Require student response at each step.
  • Provide immediate feedback/reinforcement.
Advantages:
  • Self-paced & Consistent instruction.
  • Immediate feedback on progress.
  • Easy to monitor student progress.
Disadvantages:
  • Limited interactivity & human contact.
  • Can be repetitive/boring.
  • Not suitable for complex/nuanced topics.
Creative Thinking Strategies: Generating innovative ideas...
Description:

Various methods and techniques used to help individuals generate and develop new and innovative ideas beyond standard solutions.

Implementation Steps:
  • Choose a technique (e.g., Mind Mapping).
  • Define the central problem or theme.
  • Branch out ideas or connect unrelated concepts.
  • Refine and develop the most promising ideas.
Common Methods:
  • Brainstorming: Rapid idea generation without judgment.
  • Mind Mapping: Visual representation of connections.
  • Forced Connections: Connecting unrelated concepts.

Lesson Planning Structure

Objectives
Define the learning outcomes learners are expected to achieve by the end of the lesson.
Values and Life Skills
Identify the values and life skills promoted through the lesson, such as teamwork, critical thinking, communication, responsibility, or creativity.
Resources
List the materials and tools used during the lesson, such as textbooks, worksheets, visual aids, digital resources, or real-life examples.
Strategies
Specify the teaching and learning strategies applied in the lesson, including discussion, cooperative learning, inquiry-based learning, demonstration, or differentiated instruction.
Review
Summarize and reinforce the main points of the lesson to ensure understanding and retention.
Warm-up
Introduce an engaging activity to activate prior knowledge and motivate learners toward the lesson topic.
Presentation
Explain and demonstrate the new content in a clear and structured manner using appropriate methods and examples.
Vocabulary / Key Terms
Introduce and clarify essential terms and concepts required for understanding the lesson.
Practice
Provide opportunities for learners to apply new knowledge and skills through guided and independent activities.
Assessment
Assess learners‚Äô achievement of the lesson objectives using appropriate formative or summative assessment methods.
Assignment
Provide follow-up work to reinforce learning, extend understanding, or prepare for upcoming lessons.
